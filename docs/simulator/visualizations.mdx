## Panel 1: Two-Box Ocean Diagram (Top-Left)

### What It Shows

A simplified schematic representation of the current state of the model:
- Two boxes representing equatorial (E) and polar (P) regions
- Colors encode temperature and salinity
- Arrows represent circulation between boxes
- Animated arrows show flow direction and strength in real time

### How to Read It

**Box colors**:
- **Red**: Warm, salty water (equatorial box)
- **Blue**: Cold, fresh water (polar box)
- **Intermediate shades**: In-between states

**Arrow representation**:
- **Thick northward arrow**: Strong positive circulation (q > 10 Sv) — typical AMOC
- **Thin arrow**: Weak circulation (0 < q < 5 Sv)
- **Reversed arrow**: Negative circulation (q < 0) — rare, haline-dominated regime
- **No visible arrow**: Circulation near zero (collapsed state)

**Animation**:
- Arrows animate continuously, giving a dynamic sense of the flow rate
- Thickness and animation speed correspond to |q|
- Direction (north or south) corresponds to sign of q

**Label indicators** (bottom of diagram):
- "Thermally Driven": q > 10 Sv, strong AMOC
- "Neutral": q ≈ 0, barely flowing
- "Weakened": 2 < q < 8 Sv, visible but reduced
- "Collapsed": q ≈ 0 or negative
- "Reversed": q < 0, haline-dominated

### Interpretation

This is the **most intuitive** visualization. If you only glanced at one panel, this tells the story:
- Wide northward arrows = healthy AMOC
- Thin arrows = circulation failing
- Reversed arrows = catastrophe

Use this to get a quick visual sense of the system state without reading numbers.

## Panel 2: AMOC Comparison (Top-Right)

### What It Shows

A bar chart comparing:
- **Left bar (blue)**: Your simulation's final circulation strength q_sv in Sverdrups
- **Right bar (grey)**: Observed AMOC mean from the RAPID-MOCHA array (16.9 ± 4.4 Sv)
- **Error bars**: Standard deviation (±4.4 Sv) shown on RAPID bar

### How to Read It

**Color coding**:
- **Dark blue**: Normal AMOC (~15 Sv), good match to observations
- **Orange**: Weakened AMOC (~5–10 Sv), below observed mean
- **Bright red**: Collapsed AMOC (~0 Sv), dramatically different from observations

**Scale**:
- Y-axis shows Sverdrups (Sv), standard oceanographic unit
- Dashed horizontal lines at 0, 5, 10, 15, 20 Sv for reference

### Interpretation

**What it tells you**:
1. **Is your model realistic?** Comparing to RAPID-MOCHA data grounds your simulation in observations
2. **How far from observations?** If your bar is outside the ±4.4 Sv error band, your forcing is likely extreme
3. **What regime are you in?**
   - Strongly blue: Present-day or past strong-AMOC state
   - Orange: Future weakening scenario
   - Red: Collapse scenario (not observed recently, but possible)

**Example interpretation**:
- Load "Normal AMOC" preset → bar aligns with RAPID mean → realistic
- Load "Collapsed AMOC" preset → bar near zero → outside observed range (collapse state)

### What It Doesn't Show

- **Uncertainty in the model**: The simulator doesn't account for parameterization uncertainties
- **Variability over time**: Only shows final state, not time-dependent behavior
- **Other AMOC indices**: RAPID-MOCHA is just one observational product

## Panel 3: Time Series (Full-Width Middle Row)

### What It Shows

Three time series plotted against time (x-axis, in years):
1. **Circulation q(t)** (blue line, left y-axis): In Sverdrups, showing how q evolves
2. **Box 1 / equatorial salinity S₁(t)** (yellow/orange line, right y-axis): In psu
3. **Box 2 / polar salinity S₂(t)** (cyan line, right y-axis): In psu

### How to Read It

**Time axis**:
- Ranges from 0 to t_max (default 3000 years)
- Represents one full simulation run

**Left y-axis (blue, Sv)**:
- Shows circulation strength
- Range: typically -5 to +20 Sv
- **Red dashed line at q = 0**: Marks collapse threshold
- **Grey dashed line at q ≈ 15 Sv**: Reference line (present-day AMOC)

**Right y-axis (psu)**:
- Shows salinity (practical salinity units)
- Range: typically 33–37 psu

**Trajectory shape**:
- **Flat (after initial transient)**: System in equilibrium; stable state
- **Gradual curve**: System approaching equilibrium; slow dynamics
- **Sudden jump**: Bifurcation or noise-induced transition

### Interpretation Examples

**Scenario A: Normal AMOC**
```
Time series shape:
q: starts at ~10 Sv → quickly rises → flattens at ~15 Sv (equilibrium)
S₁: starts at 36 → slightly decreases → flat at ~36.2 psu
S₂: starts at 34 → slightly increases → flat at ~34.8 psu
Interpretation: System quickly equilibrates to strong stable state
```

**Scenario B: Weakened AMOC**
```
Time series shape:
q: starts at ~10 Sv → slowly decreases → flattens at ~5 Sv (after ~2000 years)
S₁: gradual decrease → flat at lower value
S₂: gradual increase → flat at higher value
Interpretation: Slower equilibration (critical slowing down); marginal state
```

**Scenario C: Collapsed AMOC**
```
Time series shape:
q: starts at ~10 Sv → rapidly drops → flat near 0 or negative (within ~500 years)
S₁: rapid decrease to lower value
S₂: rapid increase to higher value
Interpretation: Bifurcation/jump to collapsed state; irreversible transition
```

### What to Look For

1. **Equilibration time**: How long until the curves flatten?
   - <500 years = far from bifurcation
   - 1000–2000 years = near bifurcation (critical slowing down)
   - >2000 years = very close to tipping

2. **Relaxation toward equilibrium**: Each box's salinity is pulled toward its target S₁ᵉᵍ / S₂ᵉᵍ at rate λ (~1000-year timescale). Total salt is **not** conserved in this relaxation model.

3. **Circulation-salinity coupling**:
   - Stronger q (more northward flow) → more salt transported north → larger S₁, smaller S₂
   - Weaker q → less salt transport → smaller ΔS

## Panel 4: Phase Space Portrait (Bottom-Left)

### What It Shows

A 2D plot of the system trajectory in **state space**:
- **X-axis**: Salinity difference ΔS = S₁ − S₂ (psu)
- **Y-axis**: Circulation strength q (Sv)
- **Colored line**: Trajectory from initial state (red star) to final state (green circle)
- **Color gradient**: Blue = early time, Yellow = late time (viridis colormap)
- **Dashed line**: The q-nullcline (equilibrium curve where dq/dt = 0)

### How to Read It

**Initial & final states**:
- **Red star**: Starting condition (S₁ᵢⁿⁱᵗ, S₂ᵢⁿⁱᵗ)
- **Green circle**: Final state (S₁*, S₂*) — where the system ends up
- **Distance between them**: How far the system "moved" in state space

**The q-nullcline** (dashed line):
- This is the curve relating q to ΔS by the diagnostic equation
- Equation: q = k[α·ΔT − β·ΔS]
- The nullcline is a **straight line** with negative slope (higher ΔS → weaker q if ΔT is fixed)
- The system's equilibria lie at the intersection of this line with the salinity-relaxation balance
- The trajectory **asymptotically approaches** equilibrium

**Trajectory shape**:
- **Spiral**: System "overshoots" then settles on the nullcline
- **Straight**: Direct approach to equilibrium (rare)
- **Jump**: Bifurcation or noise-induced transition

### Interpretation Examples

**Scenario A: Strong AMOC (Normal AMOC preset)**
```
Phase space:
Red star at ΔS ≈ 2 psu, q ≈ 10 Sv (upper branch)
Trajectory spirals slightly, then settles on nullcline
Green circle at ΔS ≈ 2 psu, q ≈ 15 Sv (final equilibrium, upper branch)
Interpretation: Starts near equilibrium; minor transient; stable attractor
```

**Scenario B: Weakened AMOC (Weakened AMOC preset)**
```
Phase space:
Red star at ΔS ≈ 2 psu, q ≈ 10 Sv
Trajectory approaches nullcline very slowly (spiral is flat, diffuse)
Green circle at ΔS ≈ 1 psu, q ≈ 5 Sv (still upper branch but closer to fold)
Interpretation: Critical slowing down; trajectory is sluggish; close to bifurcation
```

**Scenario C: Collapsed AMOC (Collapsed AMOC preset)**
```
Phase space:
Red star at ΔS ≈ 2 psu, q ≈ 10 Sv (upper branch)
Trajectory **jumps** across nullcline to lower branch
Green circle at ΔS ≈ -0.5 psu, q ≈ 0 Sv (lower branch, collapsed)
Interpretation: Bifurcation occurred; system "escaped" upper branch basin of attraction
```

### Basins of Attraction

In the bistable region, the nullcline splits into three parts:
- **Upper branch** (stable, upper right): Strong AMOC attractor
- **Middle branch** (unstable, middle): Separatrix between basins
- **Lower branch** (stable, lower left): Weak/collapsed attractor

The **phase space portrait shows which basin you're in**:
- Red star and green circle both on upper → you stayed in strong AMOC basin
- Red star on upper, green circle on lower → you tipped/transitioned

## Panel 5: Bifurcation Diagram (Bottom-Right)

### What It Shows

*Only appears after clicking "Compute Bifurcation Diagram".*

A 2D plot showing equilibrium circulation **q** as a function of temperature difference **ΔT = T₁ − T₂**:
- **X-axis**: ΔT (°C), swept by varying T₂ (default range: T₂ ∈ [0, 20] °C → ΔT ∈ [5, 25] °C)
- **Y-axis**: Equilibrium circulation q_sv (Sv), ranging from -5 to +20
- **Blue upper branch**: Stable states from forward sweep (T₂ low → high, ΔT high → low)
- **Orange lower branch**: Stable states from backward sweep (T₂ high → low, ΔT low → high), starting from a fresh polar condition
- **Grey shaded region**: Bistable region (hysteresis window)
- **Red diamonds**: Bifurcation points (collapse and recovery thresholds)
- **Orange X marker**: Your current ΔT from the T₁/T₂ sliders

### How to Read It

**The S-curve**:
- Resembles a sideways "S" or fold, plotted against ΔT
- **Forward branch** (upper, blue): traces strong-AMOC equilibria as ΔT decreases until collapse at ΔT_crit
- **Backward branch** (lower, orange): traces weak/collapsed equilibria as ΔT increases until recovery
- **Gap between upper and lower** = hysteresis window; two states coexist here for the same ΔT

**Bifurcation points** (red diamonds):
- **Collapse threshold (small ΔT)**: Where the upper (strong-AMOC) branch dies
  - For ΔT > collapse threshold: upper branch is stable
  - For ΔT < collapse threshold: only lower branch is stable
- **Recovery threshold (larger ΔT)**: Where the lower (collapsed) branch dies
  - For ΔT > recovery threshold: both branches stable (bistable)
  - For ΔT > a still-larger value beyond the diamond: only upper branch stable

**The orange X marker**:
- Shows your current ΔT (from T₁ − T₂) on the diagram
- Moves left/right as you adjust the T₁ or T₂ sliders
- Falls in the "bistable window" if it sits between the two diamonds

### Interpretation Examples

**Scenario A: Wide hysteresis (default Stommel parameters)**
```
Bifurcation diagram:
Collapse threshold ΔT_c ≈ 8–12°C
Recovery threshold ΔT_r ≈ 12–17°C
Hysteresis width ≈ 5°C in ΔT
Interpretation: Large "memory" — system can remain in one state for a wide range of ΔT
```

**Scenario B: Narrow hysteresis (smaller β)**
```
Bifurcation diagram:
Both thresholds shift; hysteresis window shrinks
Interpretation: Smaller bistable region; less history dependence; easier to predict
```

### Why Compute Bifurcation?

- **Understanding stability**: See the full picture of all possible steady states
- **Finding tipping points**: Exactly identify the collapse and recovery thresholds
- **Designing experiments**: Plan which T₂ values to run based on diagram
- **Parameter sensitivity**: Recompute after changing parameters to see how tipping points shift

## Panel 6: Parameter Controls (Left Side)

### What It Shows

All sliders, buttons, and options for controlling the simulation, organized into collapsible sections:
- **Preset buttons** (top): Normal AMOC, Weakened, Collapsed, Stommel 1961
- **Temperature & Salinity** (open by default): T₁, T₂, S₁ᵉᵍ, S₂ᵉᵍ, S₁ᵢⁿⁱᵗ, S₂ᵢⁿⁱᵗ
- **Physics Parameters** (open by default): α, β, k (log), λ (log)
- **Stochastic Forcing** (collapsed): noise_amplitude
- **Integration** (collapsed): t_max, dt
- **Action buttons** at bottom: Run Simulation (green), Reset (red), Compute Bifurcation Diagram (blue)

### How to Use It

1. **Adjust sliders** to set parameters
2. **Click "Run Simulation"** to execute with current parameters
3. **Watch all 5 visualization panels update** with new results
4. **Click "Compute Bifurcation Diagram"** to generate the S-curve (takes ~10–30 seconds)
5. **Click "Reset"** to restore defaults

### Tips

- **No auto-run on slider change**: You must click Run Simulation each time. (The app does run once automatically when you first load the page.)
- **Log-scale sliders (k and λ)**: These use logarithmic scale; small visual changes correspond to factor-of-10 numerical changes
- **No Export button**: To get raw data, hit the API directly (see [API Reference](/api-reference/overview))

## Putting It All Together: A Complete Example

**Task**: You want to know if the current AMOC is stable or fragile.

**Step 1**: Load "Normal AMOC" preset, run simulation
- **Panel 1** (Two-Box): Thick northward arrow → visually: strong circulation
- **Panel 2** (AMOC Comparison): Blue bar aligns with RAPID mean → quantitatively: realistic
- **Panel 3** (Time Series): q flattens at ~15 Sv after a few hundred years → dynamically: stable and fast-equilibrating
- **Panel 4** (Phase Space): Red star and green circle both on upper branch → geometrically: strong basin attractor
- **Panel 5** (Bifurcation): Orange X is at ΔT = 20 °C, far from the collapse threshold → quantitatively: large safety margin

**Conclusion**: The AMOC is strong, stable, and far from tipping at this parameter set.

**Step 2**: Load "Weakened" preset (T₂ = 12), run simulation
- **Panel 1**: Thinner arrow
- **Panel 2**: Bar drops noticeably below RAPID mean
- **Panel 3**: Time series bends more slowly; takes longer to equilibrate
- **Panel 4**: Trajectory approaches equilibrium more slowly (critical slowing down)
- **Panel 5**: Orange X is now at ΔT = 13 °C, closer to the collapse diamond

**Conclusion**: The AMOC is weakened and fragile; we're approaching a tipping point.

## Troubleshooting Visualizations

| Issue | Likely Cause | Solution |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| No data in panels | Simulation hasn't run yet | Click "Run Simulation" |
| Panels blank/grey | Still running (slow computer) | Wait 10 seconds |
| Time series goes off scale | Parameters extreme (very large t_max or wild k/λ) | Reduce t_max or click Reset |
| No bifurcation diagram | "Compute Bifurcation Diagram" hasn't been clicked | Click the blue button (takes ~20 sec) |
| Phase space shows weird jump | Tipping occurred mid-simulation | Adjust T₂ or check parameters |
